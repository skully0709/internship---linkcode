I'm a Computer Science student with an MSBTE background who treats design and engineering as one discipline, not two. Fine-tuning a Figma auto-layout for an educational login screen and writing an 8086 Assembly routine both come from the same instinct — get every detail exactly right, or don't ship it.

That instinct shows up everywhere: catching a spelling error in an infographic, matching a headshot to my exact facial likeness, comparing camera specs across mid-range phones sentence by sentence. I don't work in approximations. I also don't mind long, research-heavy stretches — months of literature review on Panchayati Raj institutions, formalized into an SLA-standard report, is the kind of academic rigor I actually enjoy. And when the brief flips to "you have six hours," I can prototype a full platformer in Construct 3 and still hit the deadline.
